## References used

### 1. TWH: Burt's Concrete (neo-brutal x noir-gold)
- Hero composition: Full-bleed hero with owner pull-quote below trust row, brass left border on quote. Heavy display typography. Floating trust badge bottom-left with "20+ Years on Maui" credential.
- Section order: Hero > How We Work (process ABOVE services, process-first storytelling) > Stats strip > Services (3x2 grid with brass accent top bars) > Testimonials > Gallery > CTA > Footer
- Copy voice: Owner-operator first person angle. "Burt handles the estimate, the pour, and the finish. No subcontractors." Direct, specific, no adjective inflation.
- Photo treatment: Finished residential driveway as hero (completed work, not in-progress). Natural light, Hawaii context.
- Trust signals: Branded process name with trademark ("Grade. Form. Pour."), specific PSI numbers, joint timing specs, drainage-before-forms checklist. Crew reliability strip with quantified internal ops.
- Conversion path: Section-specific CTA wording varies (hero: estimate, warranty: site walk, prefooter: start your project). Dual CTA pattern (primary + secondary browse path).
- **Apply to this build:** Process-first storytelling (process above services), section-specific CTA wording variation, technical specs inline (PSI, rebar spacing), crew reliability strip, branded process name with trademark.

### 2. sundt.com (non-competitor, national construction firm)
- Hero composition: Construction worksite image with registered brand tagline "SKILL. GRIT. PURPOSE." Documentary photography, no staged shots.
- Section order: Workforce/people first, then hiring, then project showcases. Culture-first hierarchy unusual for construction.
- Copy voice: Working-class directness with aspirational language. Punchy, declarative sentences matching tagline rhythm. Short and rhythmic.
- Photo treatment: Active construction scenes, worker close-ups, infrastructure in natural daylight. Documentary authenticity, no renderings.
- Trust signals: Employee-ownership structure, apprenticeship programs, regional operations. Community impact narratives alongside certifications.
- Conversion path: Three-branch CTAs addressing different stakeholders. Not a single-funnel approach.
- **Apply to this build:** Branded tagline with trademark symbol, workforce/crew credibility before portfolio, documentary photo treatment, punchy declarative copy cadence, Oahu neighborhood specificity.

### Cross-reference design thesis
Lead with crew credibility and process transparency (per Reference 1's process-first order and Reference 2's workforce emphasis). Use a branded tagline with trademark ("Form. Pour. Finish.") adapted to this concrete business. Keep CTAs section-specific rather than repeating "Get a Free Quote" everywhere. Ground everything in Oahu geography with named neighborhoods and specific technical specs (4,000 PSI minimum, #4 rebar at 12-inch centers, coral aggregate soil conditions). The iron-brass palette (dark navy + bone cream + brass) provides the industrial gravity that matches concrete work.
